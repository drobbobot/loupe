# Market Landscape Research
_Prepared: 2026-02-25_

---

## 1. Overview

The Spiral Dynamics market is fragmented, specialist, and significantly underserved at the accessible end. The serious academic and practitioner infrastructure is well-developed but expensive and gated. The popular/accessible end is almost entirely audio (podcasts) with no interactive, referenceable, or structured digital product. There is no well-designed, consumer-facing SaaS product for Spiral Dynamics. This is the gap.

---

## 2. Assessments

### 2a. Official Assessments — NVC Consulting / spiraldynamics.org

**What it is:** The Values Profile II (VPII) and Change State Indicator (CSI) are the most authoritative Spiral Dynamics assessment instruments, developed in the Cowan/Todorovic lineage and administered via spiraldynamics.org.

**Cost:** Not publicly priced. Access is gated behind completing formal training — VPII becomes available to Level 1 graduates for a one-year trial period, and permanently to Level 2 graduates.

**Who it's for:** Trained SD practitioners using the tools professionally with clients. Not available to individuals or coaches without certification.

**Where it falls short:**
- Completely inaccessible to anyone who hasn't invested AUD $1,400–2,200+ in certification training first
- No self-service pathway for curious individuals, coaches, or leaders who simply want to understand themselves
- The gatekeeping is designed to prevent misuse but creates a near-total barrier to casual or exploratory use
- No modern UX — the experience is functional at best

---

### 2b. ValueMatch Assessments (bundled with training)

**What it is:** The ValueMatch Values and Change profiling report, offered as part of ValueMatch's official SDi training pathway, authorised by the Don Beck Foundation.

**Cost:** Bundled with Level 1 training (AUD $1,400–1,660 for small businesses, AUD $1,660–2,200 for corporates, excluding VAT). A standalone Certified Practitioner pathway adds AUD $1,830–2,200 at Level 2.

**Who it's for:** Coaches, OD consultants, HR professionals seeking formal certification to use SD assessments with clients.

**Where it falls short:**
- Same gatekeeping problem — not available without first committing to costly multi-week training
- Pricing and scheduling are opaque online; you must make contact to proceed
- The training is excellent but the journey from "curious" to "able to use this" is expensive and slow
- Corporate pricing signals the intended audience clearly — individual self-developers are not the target

---

### 2c. Free Informal Assessments

Several free online alternatives exist, none officially sanctioned:

| Tool | URL | Format | Quality |
|------|-----|--------|---------|
| SDTEST | sdtest.me | Free web quiz | Functional; no design; no context or coaching around results |
| Spiralize | spiralize.org/quiz | Free web quiz | Slightly better UX; still basic; no depth in results |
| The Next Evolution | thenextevolution.com | Free self-assessment | Self-described as "quick and dirty"; explicitly not for professional use |
| ValueMatch Knowledge Test | valuematch.net | Free knowledge test | Tests cognitive understanding, not personal values — a different thing entirely |

**Who they're for:** Anyone curious, with zero barrier to entry.

**Where they fall short:**
- Universally poor UX and design
- Results are a level score with minimal context — no explanation of what the level means in practice, no shadow/healthy expression nuance, no next steps
- No relational dimension — you get a personal score but no tools for understanding how your level interacts with others
- No cultural/political framing — pure individual psychology with no connection to why it matters
- Strongly prone to level inflation, with no design friction to counteract it
- None are mobile-optimised or maintained actively

---

### 2d. Cook-Greuter MAP (Maturity Assessment Profile)

**What it is:** The most empirically rigorous adult developmental assessment currently available, based on Loevinger's ego development research extended by Susanne Cook-Greuter. Uses a sentence completion format scored by trained raters.

**Cost:** Typically AUD $235–465 per assessment plus coaching debrief. Rater training is a significant investment. Used primarily in executive coaching and leadership development.

**Who it's for:** High-end executive coaches and organisational development practitioners working with senior leaders.

**Where it falls short:**
- Not Spiral Dynamics — adjacent but distinct
- High cost and logistical complexity (sentence completion requires expert scoring)
- Not accessible to individuals outside professional coaching relationships
- Not culturally grounded in the way SD is — it's a developmental score, not a worldview map

## 3. Books

### 3a. Spiral Dynamics — Beck & Cowan (1996)

**What it is:** The primary textbook. Dense, thorough, academically pitched.

**Cost:** ~AUD $50–70 new.

**Who it's for:** Practitioners and serious students. Not a casual read.

**Where it falls short:** 700+ pages of dense theory. No contemporary examples. Requires real commitment. The gap between "heard about Spiral Dynamics" and "could use this book productively" is large for most people.

---

### 3b. Trump and a Post-Truth World — Wilber (2017)

**What it is:** The most accessible and culturally compelling entry point into Spiral Dynamics as a framework for understanding political and social reality.

**Cost:** ~AUD $20–30.

**Who it's for:** Anyone curious about why the world feels so fractured. A natural entry point for people encountering SD through current events.

**Where it falls short:** Written in 2017 — examples need updating for 2026. Doesn't provide a personal growth pathway — it's diagnosis without prescription for the individual reader. Doesn't explain the full level stack in accessible depth. Brief by Wilber's standards, but still assumes some familiarity with his thinking.

---

### 3c. Reinventing Organisations — Laloux (2014)

**What it is:** The most successful popular application of Spiral Dynamics to organisational design. Uses a simplified colour model (Red, Amber, Orange, Green, Teal). Enormously influential in progressive business circles.

**Cost:** ~AUD $40–60. An illustrated summary version also exists (~AUD $60–80).

**Who it's for:** Leaders, founders, and OD practitioners interested in alternative organisational models.

**Where it falls short:**
- Only covers the organisational dimension — no personal or relational application
- The simplified five-colour model (compared to SD's eight) loses important nuance, particularly around Purple, Yellow, and Turquoise
- No assessment or interactive component
- The "Teal" aspiration has become somewhat fetishised in progressive business culture, creating unrealistic expectations

---

### 3d. A Theory of Everything — Wilber (2000)

**What it is:** The most accessible introduction to Wilber's broader framework, including his treatment of Spiral Dynamics.

**Cost:** ~AUD $24–36.

**Who it's for:** People who want the broader Integral context. A good second book after encountering SD elsewhere.

**Where it falls short:** Assumes the reader wants the full theoretical architecture — which our product direction has deliberately moved away from.

## 4. Courses and Training

### 4a. ValueMatch Level 1 & 2 Training

**What it is:** The most accessible officially certified training pathway. Eight live online sessions with instructor-led lectures and Q&A, plus self-paced online content. Leads to a Level 1 certificate from the Don Beck Foundation.

**Cost:** AUD $1,400–1,660 (Level 1), AUD $1,830–2,200 (Level 2 Certified Practitioner).

**Who it's for:** Coaches, OD consultants, HR professionals who want to use SD professionally.

**Where it falls short:** Price and time commitment rule out curious individuals and casual learners. Scheduled cohorts (not self-paced) add friction. Primarily serves the practitioner market, not the individual self-developer.

---

### 4b. Paradigm21 Level 1 Certification

**What it is:** 100% online Level 1 certification with 8 live sessions, recordings available. Authorised by the Don Beck Foundation.

**Cost:** Pricing requires website visit — not publicly listed.

**Who it's for:** Same profile as ValueMatch.

**Where it falls short:** Same gatekeeping issues. Small-scale provider with limited reach.

---

### 4c. Spiraldynamics.org — Online Principles Course

**What it is:** A 12-week online course introducing the principles within Spiral Dynamics programs. The organisation recommends a one-year Online Personal Development Track for coaches and consultants.

**Cost:** Not publicly listed.

**Who it's for:** Self-directed learners and practitioners on the Cowan/NVC lineage.

**Where it falls short:** A 12-week course plus a one-year track is a significant commitment. No pricing transparency. Website UX reflects the age of the organisation.

---

### 4d. Trace Bell — Living the Spiral

**What it is:** A course by Trace Bell (Robcast co-host, Holon Institute co-founder) going deeper into the Spiral Dynamics content from the "Me, We, Everybody" podcast series.

**Cost:** Not publicly confirmed — requires direct contact or course registration.

**Who it's for:** The audience of the Robcast — progressive spiritual seekers who encountered SD through the podcast.

**Where it falls short:** Limited reach beyond the Robcast audience. Audio-first format. Not a structured, referenceable resource. No assessment or interactive component confirmed.

---

### 4e. Phil Drysdale — YouTube Series and Community

**What it is:** A free 15-hour YouTube series covering all levels with a faith deconstruction lens. Drysdale also runs a paid private community.

**Cost:** Free (YouTube) + paid community membership (pricing undisclosed).

**Who it's for:** People deconstructing evangelical Christianity.

**Where it falls short:** Faith-specific framing is too narrow for a general audience. Video-only format. No assessment. No relational or cultural application tools.

## 5. Adjacent Frameworks and Their Digital Presence

### 5a. Teal Organisations Ecosystem

The Reinventing Organisations movement has produced several digital tools:

- **Atlas Platform** (atlas-platform.pro): A SaaS tool for organisational SD/RO assessments. Survey-based, used by OD consultants. Aimed squarely at corporate clients. Good functionality but narrow focus.
- **Reinventing Org Map** (reinvorgmap.com): Open-source visual tool mapping organisational position across multiple frameworks. Free. Aimed at practitioners.
- **Neuromap/Teal.one**: Scope-mapping tools for Teal organisations. Niche.
- **RO Community Forum**: Discussion forum for Teal practitioners. Active but specialist.

**Overall assessment:** The Teal ecosystem has more digital tooling than the SD ecosystem, but it's entirely organisational — no individual or cultural application. The UX is functional rather than engaging.

---

### 5b. Barrett Values Centre

**What it is:** The Cultural Transformation Tools (CTT), including the Personal Values Assessment (PVA) and Cultural Values Assessment (CVA). Widely used in leadership development and cultural transformation.

**Cost:** PVA is free online. Full organisational assessments require Barrett consultant certification, which is a significant investment.

**Who it's for:** HR professionals and OD consultants doing cultural transformation work with organisations.

**Where it falls short:** Not Spiral Dynamics — it maps values but not developmental levels. The link to SD requires the practitioner to make it. Not accessible to individuals for self-development purposes.

---

### 5c. Center for Action and Contemplation (Richard Rohr)

**What it is:** Richard Rohr's CAC publishes accessible content on Spiral Dynamics and developmental spirituality, framing it within a contemplative Christian context.

**Cost:** Free (online articles, podcast). Daily Meditations email list is free.

**Who it's for:** Spiritually oriented adults interested in developmental frameworks.

**Where it falls short:** Entirely content-based — no assessment, no interactive tools, no structured learning pathway. Faith context limits general appeal.

---

### 5d. Robert Kegan / Immunity to Change

**What it is:** Kegan and Lahey's Immunity to Change methodology has a well-developed coaching practice and book. The ITC Map is used in executive development.

**Cost:** Book ~AUD $40. ITC coaching is expensive — typically embedded in leadership development programmes.

**Who it's for:** Leaders and executive coaches.

**Where it falls short:** Not Spiral Dynamics — adjacent but different. No consumer-facing digital product.

---

### 5e. 4 Lenses™ (Shipley Communication)

**What it is:** A colour-coded personality and temperament assessment developed by Shipley Communication, derived from the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator and David Keirsey's temperament research. Divides people into four temperament types, each assigned a colour: **Gold** (structure, duty, tradition), **Blue** (relationships, harmony, empathy), **Green** (logic, ideas, analysis), and **Orange** (action, spontaneity, freedom). Used extensively in corporate team-building, communication training, and leadership development. Several million users globally. The primary book is *Four Lenses Unfolded* by Nathan K. Price.

**Cost:** Assessment typically delivered as part of facilitated workshops; individual access via fourlenses.com. Pricing varies by delivery model — corporate workshop packages are the primary vehicle.

**Who it's for:** HR professionals, team leaders, corporate trainers, and organisations investing in team communication and interpersonal effectiveness.

**Where it falls short:**
- A horizontal typology, not a developmental model. The four types are static — you're a Gold or an Orange, permanently, rather than being somewhere on a developmental journey. There is no arc, no growth direction, no shadow expressed as a developmental challenge.
- Shares colour language with Spiral Dynamics (Blue, Green, Orange appear in both) but describes entirely different things. This risks genuine confusion for users who have encountered both.
- Explains *how* people differ in style but not *why* — no framework for understanding the life conditions or worldview logic that produces a given profile.
- No cultural or political dimension — purely interpersonal and organisational.
- No shadow/dysfunction content — the model is essentially positive, treating each type as a valid style with no serious treatment of what each looks like under stress or in unhealthy expression.

**Competitive relevance to our product:**
The 4 Lenses is a significant indirect competitor — it occupies the "understand why people are different" space in corporate and team contexts, is widely known, and is far more accessible than any SD product. The risk is that potential users satisfied with a simpler typology don't feel the need to go deeper. The opportunity is that users who find 4 Lenses insufficient — because it doesn't explain *development*, doesn't address cultural and political dynamics, and doesn't offer a growth trajectory — are a natural pipeline into our product.

**Naming note:** Our use of "lens/lenses" as primary user-facing language creates a potential association with the 4 Lenses brand. This should be factored into naming decisions — either we distinguish clearly, or we explore whether proximity to a known brand is a feature (recognition) or a risk (confusion). Recommend testing with target users before committing to "lenses" as a primary product term.

## 7. The Top 5 Underserved Gaps

### Gap 1: No beautiful, accessible consumer product for Spiral Dynamics

There is no well-designed, consumer-facing digital product — web or mobile — that makes Spiral Dynamics genuinely accessible to a non-specialist audience. The free quizzes are functional and ugly. The official tools are locked behind training. The books require sustained commitment. The podcasts are excellent but unstructured and irreferenceable. **A designed, thoughtful SaaS product with quality UX fills an entirely empty space.**

---

### Gap 2: No product that connects Me → We → Everybody in one place

Every existing resource addresses at most two of the three scales that Spiral Dynamics operates at simultaneously. Books cover theory (which gestures at all three but doesn't tool up the user for any). Assessments cover the individual. Org tools cover the cultural/systemic. **No single product helps a person understand their own lens, then immediately apply that understanding to their relationships, their team, and the cultural and political world they're living in.** The Robcast "Me / We / Everybody" framing identified this perfectly but didn't build the product.

---

### Gap 3: The shadow and unhealthy expressions are almost nowhere

Every popular resource on Spiral Dynamics focuses primarily on the healthy, positive expression of each level. The shadow — what each level looks like when it's dysfunctional, defensive, or overextended — receives minimal treatment in the accessible space. This is a major gap because the shadow is where most people's urgent questions actually live. "Why does my Blue colleague get so rigid under pressure?" "Why does my Green team collapse into consensus paralysis?" "What does my own Orange shadow look like?" **A product that takes the shadow seriously would be distinctively useful.**

---

### Gap 4: No accessible on-ramp that meets users in their current cultural moment

The Trump book is the closest thing to this but it's from 2017, it's a book, and it's politically specific. In 2026, people are living through AI disruption, institutional collapse, accelerating polarisation, and a profound sense that the old maps don't work. **There is no product that explicitly connects Spiral Dynamics to the specific texture of 2026 life** — not in an exploitative or sensationalist way, but in the grounded, "here's why this feels the way it does, and here's a more useful map" way that the best popular SD communication has always done.

---

### Gap 5: No pathway from curious individual to capable practitioner

The current market has two disconnected audiences: curious individuals (served by free, shallow content) and trained practitioners (served by expensive, gatekept tools). There is **no product that bridges these audiences** — that takes someone from "I heard about this on a podcast" through genuine understanding of the framework and into practical application in their life and work, at a price point and pace that works for an individual rather than a corporate training budget. **The conveyor belt Wilber describes doesn't exist as a product.**

---

## 8. Implications for Product Direction

These five gaps point toward a specific product opportunity: an accessible, well-designed SaaS product that:

1. Leads with the "lenses" framing and contemporary cultural relevance (Gap 4)
2. Structures itself around Me / We / Everybody use cases rather than abstract theory (Gap 2)
3. Gives genuine depth on both healthy and shadow expressions of each level (Gap 3)
4. Includes a thoughtfully designed, friction-aware self-assessment as an on-ramp (Gap 1)
5. Offers a clear, affordable pathway from curious newcomer to confident practitioner (Gap 5)

The market is not crowded. The accessible space is almost entirely empty. The main competitive risk is not existing products — it is the model itself being too niche. The product's job is to make Spiral Dynamics feel as immediately relevant and useful as any mainstream psychology framework. The evidence (Robcast listener numbers, Drysdale's reach, Wilber book sales) suggests the audience exists and is significantly larger than the existing specialist infrastructure is serving.
