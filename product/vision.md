# Product Vision Document
_Prepared: 2026-02-25_

---

## Working Title

**Loupe**

A note on the name: A loupe is a small, precision magnifying glass — the instrument a jeweller or watchmaker presses to their eye to see the true structure of something too fine for the naked eye to resolve. That's exactly what this product does: it gives people a lens precise enough to see what's actually driving human behaviour — in themselves, in the people they love, in the culture around them. The name also carries a quiet resonance with *loop* and *spiral* — the developmental arc the whole framework describes. Loupe is proposed as the confirmed product name.

---

## 1. The Problem We're Solving

Something has broken in how people relate to each other.

It's not new, but it has accelerated. Families are fractured along political lines. Friendships that survived decades are straining under the weight of incompatible worldviews. Leaders and teams talk past each other in meetings where everyone is trying their best. The gap between how different people experience reality — what feels obviously true, what feels obviously wrong — has never been more visible or more costly.

Most people have no framework for this. They interpret the gap as stupidity, bad faith, or moral failure. "How can they believe that?" is the most common question of our era, and almost nobody has a good answer. The result is either escalating conflict or exhausted withdrawal — neither of which helps.

There is a framework that explains it. Spiral Dynamics — developed by Clare W. Graves and brought to a wider audience by Don Beck, Christopher Cowan, and Ken Wilber — is a model of how human consciousness develops through a sequence of distinct worldviews, each one a genuine lens on reality. Each lens sees certain things clearly and misses others entirely. Two people operating from different lenses are not just disagreeing — they are perceiving fundamentally different realities. The framework explains why the conversations keep failing, what each person actually needs to be heard, and what movement looks like when it happens.

The problem is that this framework is almost completely inaccessible to ordinary people.

The serious resources are gated behind expensive professional training. The free tools are embarrassingly shallow. The books are dense. The podcasts that have reached a popular audience are gateways with nowhere further to go. In a world where people are desperate for a map to navigate human difference, the best map available is gathering dust in academic libraries and €900 certification programmes.

That is the gap this product fills.

## 2. Who We're Solving It For

Four distinct but overlapping audiences, united by a common underlying need:

**Jamie — The Empathic Relationship Navigator** *(primary consumer audience)*
Trying to understand why the conversations with the people they love keep failing the same way. Discovered the framework through a podcast and hit a wall trying to go deeper. Needs vivid, empathic portraits of each lens from the inside — not to judge, but to finally understand. This is the largest audience numerically and the most emotionally engaged.

**Marcus — The Curious Self-Developer** *(primary consumer audience)*
Articulate, widely-read, living through the anxieties of 2026 — AI disruption, political chaos, relational complexity. The framework gave him the most useful map he'd encountered in years. Needs a product that meets his intelligence, respects his time, and takes him somewhere the podcast didn't.

**Sofía — The Coach or OD Consultant** *(primary professional audience)*
Eight years into a coaching practice, knows SD is powerful, hasn't formalised it yet. Needs professional-grade tools she can use with clients — an assessment she'd actually send, language she can borrow, a practitioner layer that makes her more effective. Will pay a professional tier and will refer colleagues.

**Priya — The Culture-Aware Leader** *(secondary professional audience)*
CEO or senior leader who encountered SD through organisational culture work. Needs a strategic tool for understanding her team and her own blind spots — not a certification programme, but a serious, independent resource that's credible enough to use in a leadership context.

**The common thread:** all four arrived here through a specific experience of confusion or pain — not intellectual curiosity. All four need a product that earns their trust before it asks for their attention. All four are living in the same attention economy and will not tolerate tedium. All four are reaching for the relational dimension and finding nothing adequate.

## 3. Our Unique Angle

Three things make this product distinct from everything that currently exists:

**We lead with lenses, not levels.** Every existing product — from free quizzes to certification programmes — frames Spiral Dynamics as a hierarchy of levels to ascend. This framing is both intellectually misleading and practically counterproductive. It produces self-inflation, dismissiveness toward others, and identity capture. We frame the model the way Wilber does in *Trump and a Post-Truth World*: as a set of lenses, each of which sees something real that the others don't. This is more honest, more accessible, and more useful for the relational use case that drives most of our audience.

**We take the shadow seriously.** Every accessible resource on Spiral Dynamics focuses on the healthy, positive expression of each lens. The shadow — what each lens looks like when it's frightened, closed-down, or overextended — is almost entirely absent from popular SD content. This is precisely where the most urgent questions live: why does my Blue parent get so rigid and punitive under stress? What does my own Orange shadow look like when I'm running it on other people? We treat the shadow with the same depth and compassion as the healthy expression, and this is a clear, unoccupied differentiator.

**We are structured around the Me/We/Everybody journey.** The Robcast's "Me, We, Everybody" framing maps directly onto the developmental arc of the spiral itself: Me (Beige through Red — self-centric, survival-oriented, tribal) becomes We (Blue and Orange — group, institution, and achievement) becomes Everybody (Green and beyond — universal, pluralistic, holistic). This isn't three scales of application — it's the human developmental journey itself. We organise the product around this arc because it gives users an immediate, intuitive orientation to where they are and where the road goes, without needing to memorise eight colour names before they understand anything.

## 4. Core Value Proposition

**Loupe helps you understand the lens you see the world through — and the lenses the people around you are using — so that the conversations that matter most stop failing the same way.**

For the consumer (Jamie, Marcus): *Finally understand why people you love see the world so differently — and what to actually do about it.*

For the professional (Sofía, Priya): *The framework that explains what's really happening in your team, your clients, and yourself — built into a tool you'd actually use.*

The product is not a personality test. It is not a ranking of human worth. It is a map of human difference that makes confusion intelligible, judgment optional, and genuine connection more possible.

---

## 5. Product Principles

These principles govern every design and content decision. They are non-negotiable.

**Lenses, not levels.** Never present the framework as a hierarchy of human worth. Always present it as a spectrum of genuine, valid ways of perceiving reality. The language throughout is "lens" not "stage" or "level" in user-facing contexts.

**Empathy before analysis.** Every portrait of a lens must work to generate genuine empathy for people operating from that lens before it offers analytical tools. Understanding comes first; application second.

**The shadow earns equal treatment.** Healthy and unhealthy expressions of each lens receive equivalent depth of treatment. The shadow is not a warning label — it is where the most self-aware users are looking.

**Depth in layers, never upfront.** The entry experience must be immediately engaging without requiring any prior knowledge. Depth is available on demand, not imposed. Nobody should feel overwhelmed in their first session.

**Engagement is structural, not decorative.** The product competes with social media for attention. Content units are short, ideas land fast, interactions create surprise and forward momentum. This is not a course to be completed — it is a tool to be returned to.

**Resist inflation by design.** The assessment is built to surface honest results rather than flattering ones. Friction is intentional. The goal is a result users recognise, not one they aspire to.

**No jargon at the surface.** Technical terms (vMEME, ECLET, First Tier, Second Tier) are available in depth layers for those who want them. The surface language is plain, vivid, and human.

**The conveyor belt runs in both directions.** The product supports both forward movement (growth, expanding perspective) and honest acknowledgment that under stress, everyone contracts. Meeting users where they are means meeting them in both directions.

## 6. Prioritised Feature Set

Features are grouped into three tiers: Core (MVP essential), Extended (post-MVP priority), and Future (longer horizon).

### Tier 1 — Core (MVP)

**The Lens Assessment**
A thoughtful, engaging self-assessment that identifies a user's current centre of gravity across the spectrum. Designed with friction to resist inflation — asks indirect, situational questions rather than obvious self-report items. Produces a result that includes: primary lens, secondary lens, shadow tendencies at the primary lens, and a brief description of what growth looks like from here. Not a type — explicitly framed as a current centre of gravity that is contextual and can shift.

**The Lens Library**
Eight richly written, visually distinct lens profiles covering the full spectrum from Beige to Turquoise. Grouped within the Me/We/Everybody developmental arc — Me lenses (Beige, Purple, Red), We lenses (Blue, Orange), Everybody lenses (Green, Yellow, Turquoise) — so users have an immediate orientation to where each lens sits in the journey. Each profile includes: what the world genuinely looks and feels like from inside this lens (first-person voice), what this lens values and why those values make complete sense, the healthy gifts this lens offers, the shadow expressions when this lens is under stress or closed-down, real-world examples in relationships, work, and culture, and how to recognise and genuinely connect with someone operating from this lens. Written to generate empathy first and analysis second. Short, punchy, designed for skimming with rewards for reading deeply.

**Your Profile**
The user's personal lens portrait: their centre of gravity, their shadow tendencies, and a grounded guide to what their current lens means for how they show up — in relationships, under stress, and when growing. Draws directly from their assessment result and the Lens Library. Explicitly frames the result as a current home base, not a fixed identity. Includes orientation to what the journey forward looks like from where they are.

**Navigating Relationships**
Relational lens dynamics: how specific lens combinations play out in real relationships, what tends to go wrong between them, and what genuine connection across difference looks like. Written for the actual relationships users are living — not abstract pairings. Prioritises the most common and most painful combinations: adult children and parents, partners with different centres of gravity, friendships fractured along political or values lines, and the workplace dynamics that erode team trust.

**The World Right Now**
A contextual layer connecting the framework to the cultural and political moment of 2026. Draws directly on the Wilber/*Trump and a Post-Truth World* approach: here is what is happening, here is the lens-logic underneath it, here is why it keeps producing the same patterns. Updated periodically. Makes the framework feel urgently relevant rather than abstractly interesting — this is the feature that earns the product a place in conversations about real events, not just self-development.

---

### Tier 2 — Extended (Post-MVP Priority)

**Organisations and Culture**
Tools and content for understanding lens dynamics at the team and organisational scale: how a team's spread of lenses shapes its decision-making, conflict patterns, and capacity for change. Aimed at Priya and Sofía. Includes a team mapping tool, content on how organisations develop and get stuck, and guidance on leading change across different lens orientations.

**The Practitioner Layer**
An additional interface layer for users like Sofía — coaching notes for each lens, conversation prompts, what to watch for, common misidentifications. Assessment sharing with clients. A way to hold multiple client profiles. Accessed at a professional subscription tier.

**Lens Combinations deep-dive**
Expanded content for the most common and most difficult relational combinations — a full guide to each pairing rather than brief signposts. Particularly: adult child / parent combinations (Jamie's use case), team dynamics, and romantic partnership friction.

**Growth pathway**
A structured but self-paced learning journey for users who want to go deeper — from foundational understanding through relational application through cultural fluency. Designed as the conveyor belt: no cliff between "heard about this on a podcast" and "confident practitioner."

**Shadow work module**
Dedicated, deeper content on each lens's shadow — what it looks like in behaviour, what usually triggers it, what moving out of it requires. Written with compassion, not judgment. One of the most differentiated pieces of content the product can offer.

---

### Tier 3 — Future

**Team mapping tool** — for leaders like Priya: a structured way to map a leadership team's lens distribution and understand dynamics, decision-making patterns, and change readiness.

**Assessment for others** — guided process for building a hypothesis about someone else's lens based on observed behaviour, for use in coaching, leadership, and personal relationships.

**Progress and reflection** — lightweight journaling and check-in tools: how am I showing up this week? What lens am I running? Where am I contracting?

**Community and cohorts** — connection with others at similar centres of gravity, or working on similar relational challenges. Carefully moderated to prevent community from becoming a level-hierarchy status game.

**API / practitioner integrations** — for coaches and OD consultants who want to embed assessment and content in their own client workflows.

## 7. What This Product Is Not

As important as what it is:

- **Not a personality test.** It does not assign a fixed type. Results are explicitly framed as a current centre of gravity that shifts with context and growth.
- **Not a ranking system.** No lens is presented as superior. The language of "higher" and "lower" does not appear in user-facing content.
- **Not a course.** There is no linear completion path, no certificates, no pressure to finish. It is a reference tool and a thinking companion.
- **Not therapy.** It offers frameworks for understanding, not clinical support. Where content touches on difficult relational material, the product is clear about its limits.
- **Not a political product.** The cultural analysis is grounded and fair. It applies the framework to all sides of contemporary culture wars with equal honesty. It does not have a political agenda.

## 8. Business Model (Preliminary)

**Personal tier** — monthly/annual subscription for individual users (Marcus, Jamie). Access to assessment, full lens library, Me and We sections, current world framing. Price point: accessible, comparable to a quality newsletter subscription (~AUD $16–24/month).

**Professional tier** — monthly/annual subscription for coaches, consultants, and practitioners (Sofía). Includes practitioner layer, client assessment sharing, multiple profiles, conversation guides. Price point: professional tool (~AUD $60–100/month).

**Team/organisational tier** — annual licence for leaders and organisations (Priya). Team mapping tools, organisational culture diagnostics, bulk assessment, custom reporting. Price point: B2B, per-seat or flat annual (~AUD $1,000–4,000/year depending on team size).

**Growth pathway (optional add-on)** — a deeper, structured learning journey for users who want to go beyond the core product. One-time purchase or included in annual personal/professional plans.

## 9. Success Looks Like

In 12 months:
- Users report that the product genuinely changed how they understand a specific relationship
- Coaches recommend it to colleagues and use it independently with clients
- The shadow content is specifically cited as the most valuable and distinctive part of the product
- Engagement patterns show users returning to the We section repeatedly, not just completing the assessment once
- The product earns coverage in the same circles as the Robcast and Wilber's popular work — self-development, progressive spirituality, leadership, therapy-adjacent communities

In 3 years:
- Loupe is the standard reference tool for practitioners working with Spiral Dynamics
- The product has materially grown the accessible SD audience — people who hadn't heard of the framework discovered it here
- The conveyor belt is working: a meaningful percentage of personal-tier users have moved to professional tier
