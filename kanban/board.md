## Product Direction Flags
- **FLAG-1:** REVISED: Product uses Wilber's framing from *Trump and a Post-Truth World* as its lens. Accessibility is the priority. AQAL is background knowledge only.
- **FLAG-2:** Assessment design must resist level inflation. Friction by design.
- **FLAG-3:** "Lenses" framing is primary user-facing language.
- **FLAG-4:** Cultural relevance to 2026 (AI disruption, polarisation, post-truth) is a positioning opportunity.
- **FLAG-5:** Tone benchmark: Rob & Trace Bell's "Me, We, Everybody."
- **FLAG-6:** The accessible SaaS space is effectively empty. Main risk is niche appeal, not competition.
- **FLAG-7:** Shadow/unhealthy expressions are underserved — clear differentiator.
- **FLAG-8:** No conveyor belt from curious newcomer → confident practitioner exists. Unoccupied.
- **FLAG-9:** Four cross-persona patterns: credibility bar is high; shadow is the most urgent unmet need; relational dimension is what makes it practically useful; entry point is emotional not intellectual.
- **FLAG-10:** Engagement is a delivery constraint, not a UX nicety. Content must be layered, immediate, and interactive — tedium is an exit event at every screen.
